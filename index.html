An Analysis of Community Characteristics and Wage Theft Prevalence

Division of Data Analytics

Employees Owed Back Wages

  • Each year, WHD finds back wages for tens of thousands of employees
  • Addresses are collected for the majority
  • What are the characteristics of the communities in which these employees live?
  • Can we utilize this information to refine our targeting strategies?

Analysis Overview

  • Retrieve employees addresses from BWFS for FY20 - FY22
  • Geocode addresses to identify Census geographies in which employees live
  • Utilizing 2016 - 2020 American Community Survey (ACS) data, estimate the following for each public use microdata area (PUMA):
  • Unemployment rate
  • Median educational attainment
  • Wage and salary earnings at the 10th percentile
  • Proportion of workers that are Black
  • Proportion of workers that are Hispanic
  • Proportion of workers that are not White
  • Total population

Analysis Overview

  • Combine employee address data with ACS data
  • Compute the number of employees found due back wages per 10,000 in each PUMA
  • Identify correlations between employees due back wages per capita and community characteristics
